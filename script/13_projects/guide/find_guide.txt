# Linux `find` Command — Simple Cheat Sheet

## Basic Syntax
find [path] [conditions] [action]

Example:
find . -name "file.txt"

---

## Search by Name
find . -name "file.txt"        # Exact name
find . -iname "*.jpg"          # Case-insensitive
find . -name "*.log"           # Wildcard match

---

## Search by Type
find . -type f     # Files only
find . -type d     # Directories only
find . -type l     # Symlinks

---

## Search by Size
find . -size +100M   # Larger than 100MB
find . -size -10k    # Smaller than 10KB
find . -size 50M     # Exactly 50MB

Units:
k = KB  
M = MB  
G = GB  

---

## Search by Time
find . -mtime -7     # Modified in last 7 days
find . -mtime +30    # Older than 30 days
find . -mmin -10     # Modified in last 10 minutes

---

## Search by User / Permission
find . -user john
find . -perm 755

---

## Delete Files (Be Careful)
find . -name "*.log" -delete

Safer alternative:
find . -name "*.log" -exec rm {} +

---

## Run Command on Results
find . -name "*.txt" -exec ls -l {} \;
find . -name "*.txt" -exec ls -l {} +

---

## Logical Operators
find . -type f -size +10M                # AND (default)
find . -name "*.jpg" -o -name "*.png"    # OR
find . ! -name "*.txt"                   # NOT

Grouping:
find . \( -name "*.jpg" -o -name "*.png" \)

---

## Limit Search Depth
find . -maxdepth 1
find . -mindepth 2

---

## Common Real-World Examples
find . -type f -size +1G
find /var/log -type f -mtime +30
find . -type d -empty
find / -name "config.php" 2>/dev/null

---

## Memory Formula
find WHERE WHAT ACTION

Example:
find /home -type f -size +100M -delete
